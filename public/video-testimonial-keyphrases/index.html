Short-Form Video Hooks

Short-form (Reels, TikTok, Shorts) is algorithm-driven, not search-driven. The keyphrase here becomes your opening hook — the first 3 seconds. These hooks align with the same high-intent search clusters above, so they attract the right audience organically.
Hook A · Dyslexia Angle
"My dyslexic child couldn't read a single word. Here's what finally changed."
Targets → reading program for dyslexia (1,300/mo)
Hook B · Parent Transformation
"I spent two years trying to teach my kid to read. Then I found this."
Targets → how to teach my child to read (1,000/mo)
Hook C · Comparison / Best-Of
"We tried every reading program. This one was actually different."
Targets → best learn to read program (140/mo)
Hook D · Results-First
"3 months with Reading Horizons — here are our actual results."
Targets → reading horizons review (40/mo, LOW competition)
Hook E · Emotional Stakes
"Reading time used to end in tears every single night. Not anymore."
Targets → struggling reader audience → dyslexia cluster
Hook F · Educator / School
"My students were years behind in reading. This is what we used to catch them up."
Targets → reading intervention program audience

High Priority
Lead with the dyslexia angle
Even if the subject isn't dyslexic, a struggling reader story maps perfectly to this cluster. "Reading program for dyslexia" gets 1,300/mo with KD 3–17 — the single largest opportunity.

Create a standalone Review video
"Reading Horizons Review" pulls 40 branded searches/mo with almost no competing videos. Anyone already in the funnel will find it. This is your easiest win on YouTube.
03
Medium Priority
Build transformation hooks for short-form
Pair 60-second emotional clips with the hashtag clusters above. Short-form feeds the YouTube algorithm and gets surfaced to the right audience passively, compounding over time.
04
Medium Priority
Target the parent question keyword
"How to teach my child to read" (1,000/mo, KD 23) is a question-form query that YouTube surfaces strongly for how-to and informational video. Build a parent-focused testimonial around this phrasing.
